# Calibration & importance

A prediction interval is only useful if it means what it says, and a model is only trustworthy if you can see *why* it decides. The machine layer checks both: conformal calibration on the quantile band, and dual (SHAP + permutation) feature importance.

---

## Does the 80% interval actually cover 80%?

The degradation quantile trio claims an 80% interval, `[p10, p90]`. Empirically, on the 2024 evaluation set (n = 18,677):

| Measure | Coverage |
|---|---|
| Nominal target | 0.800 |
| Raw `[p10, p90]` empirical coverage | **0.803** |
| Split-conformal (CQR) coverage | **0.805** |
| Mean interval width | 1.106 s |

The raw quantiles are **already calibrated** empirical coverage lands within 0.003 of nominal. A split-conformal correction (computed post-hoc on calibration/test halves of the eval set, with no extra model fits) nudges it to 0.805 with a conformal offset of essentially zero (q ≈ −0.0008). In other words, the conformal step *confirms* the calibration rather than fixing it. The coverage curve is plotted in `ml/artefacts/calibration_degradation.png`.

This is what lets strategy treat the band as honest risk: a 1.1-second-wide interval that genuinely brackets the next lap four times in five.

## Why the model decides two lenses

Feature importance is reported through **two independent methods** so agreement (or disagreement) is visible:

- **SHAP** (`TreeExplainer`, 1000 rows)-game-theoretic attribution of each prediction.
- **Permutation importance**-the drop in score when a feature is shuffled.

For the median degradation regressor (`p50`), the two lenses **agree** on the top drivers:

| Rank | SHAP | Permutation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `push_residual` | `push_residual` |
| 2 | `cumulative_push_load_surface` | `cumulative_push_load_surface` |
| 3 | `age_in_stint` | `cumulative_push_load_bulk` |
| 4 | `cumulative_push_load_bulk` | `pct_drs_active` |
| 5 | `dirty_air_thermal_load_bulk` | `age_in_stint` |

Thermal load and stint age top both lists exactly the physics the [cliff problem](/machine-learning/the-cliff-problem) predicts should matter. Where the lenses diverge (e.g. `dirty_air_thermal_load_bulk` vs `pct_drs_active`), it is among correlated thermal/dirty-air features, and the disagreement is recorded in the model card rather than smoothed over.

## Ablation by feature group

Each of the nine [feature groups](/machine-learning/feature-pipeline) is dropped in turn and the score change recorded `ml/artefacts/ablation_*.parquet` for the degradation, cliff and stint-life headline models. Paired with expanding-season **learning curves** (`learning_curve_*.png`) and **partial-dependence plots** (`pdp_*.png`), this is the behaviour audit: it shows the models lean on the physically meaningful groups and keep improving as seasons are added.

A monotonicity sanity check (regressors only) compares predicted degradation against `laps_past_cliff`: p50 shows just **1 minor violation across 19 grid steps** surfaced as a finding, not constrained away.

All of these numbers are regenerated by `make ml-evaluate` into `ml/artefacts/evaluation_metrics.json`, the single feed for the [model card](/reference/ml/degradation-model-v1).
